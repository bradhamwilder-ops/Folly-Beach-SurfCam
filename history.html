History

A quick history of Folly Beach

This page gives the site a sense of place. That matters because people don’t just come back for a camera. They come back for stories, identity, and connection to the island.

Island Timeline

Simple moments worth building around

1863 to 1865

Folly Island in the Civil War era

Folly’s history reaches far beyond beach tourism. The island played a role in Civil War activity, and the Folly Beach Historical Society highlights the presence of the 54th and 55th Massachusetts and other Union troops on Folly.

1931

The original fishing pier opens

The Folly Beach Fishing Pier traces its beginnings to 1931 and later became a social landmark as well as a fishing destination.

1965

McKevlin’s enters Folly surf culture

Folly’s surf identity deepened with the opening of McKevlin’s Surf Shop, now recognized as one of the oldest surf shops in South Carolina.

1989

Hurricane Hugo helps define The Washout

The Washout’s name and modern identity are closely tied to Hurricane Hugo, which reshaped the beachfront and helped create the open break surfers still watch today.

Today

Surf, stories, tourism, and local business

Folly now blends surf culture, fishing, hospitality, and local business in a way that makes a multi-page site like this feel natural rather than forced.

Museum + Archives

History is still being preserved

The Folly Beach Historical Society and the Folly Beach History Museum continue collecting and displaying stories, photos, and surf-related local memory.
