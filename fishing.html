Fishing

Folly Beach fishing reports and keeper rules

This page gives you a place for current fishing notes, local target species, and a clean regulations snapshot for anglers checking Folly Beach, the pier, and nearby inshore water before they head out.

Current Bite

What is biting right now

Based on recent Charleston-area reports published in March 2026, the current inshore focus is redfish and speckled trout, with strong sheepshead action on nearshore reefs and structure. Around Folly, March reports also point to whiting, croaker, small black drum, sharks, rays, and bluefish showing around the surf and pier as bait becomes more active.

Best Current Inshore Target

Redfish

Recent Charleston reports describe the redfish bite as very good, especially around moving tides, with live bait and cut bait both producing.

Heating Up

Speckled Trout

Speckled trout are one of the main fish to watch as water temperatures rise and bait gets more active in creeks and grass edges.

Nearshore / Structure

Sheepshead

Current March reports around Folly and Charleston show sheepshead as one of the strongest options, especially on reefs and hard structure.

Report date context This “currently biting” summary is based on reports published on March 4, 2026 and March 12, 2026, plus recent Folly-area guide reports in February 2026.

Seasonal Targets

Best fish to target by season

This gives anglers and advertisers a quick way to understand what the page is about all year, not just this week.

Spring

Redfish, trout, sheepshead

Warming water improves redfish and trout activity, while sheepshead are a standout around structure and nearshore reefs.

Summer

Spanish, sharks, whiting, redfish

Early and late windows become important, and the beach / nearshore scene broadens with heat and bait movement.

Fall

Redfish, trout, flounder

Fall is one of the strongest all-around seasons in the Charleston area, especially for slot reds and mixed inshore action.

Winter

Redfish, trout, black drum

Winter patterns often shift toward slower presentations, schooling fish, and more deliberate creek and marsh strategies.

Regulations Snapshot

Bag limits and minimum sizes

Snapshot based on South Carolina saltwater regulations as available on March 23, 2026. Always verify before keeping fish.

Red Drum

15" to 23" TL

2 per person / 6 per boat State waters. Possession prohibited in federal waters.

Spotted Seatrout

14" TL minimum

10 per person / day Gig harvest closed Dec. 1 through Feb. 28.

Flounder

16" TL minimum

5 per person / 10 per boat Applies to hook and line or gig harvest.

Sheepshead

14" TL minimum

10 per person / 30 per boat Strong fit for dock, jetty, and structure content.

Black Drum

14" to 27" TL

5 per person / day Useful for marsh, dock, and bottom-fishing coverage.

Whiting / Spot / Croaker

No size listed

50 aggregate / person Good surf-fishing education content for visiting families.

Tripletail

18" TL minimum

3 per person / 9 per boat Seasonal nearshore interest species.

Spanish Mackerel

12" FL minimum

15 per person / day Popular warm-season nearshore and pier target.
Measurement note TL means total length. FL means fork length. Measure from closed mouth to tail according to South Carolina’s saltwater guidance.
Official source Verify limits on the South Carolina finfish limits page and through SCDNR.
